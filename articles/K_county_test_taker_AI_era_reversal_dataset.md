```yaml
core_content:

  central_observation:
    description: |
      In Chinese county-level organizations, high-credential /
      low-connection workers ("做题家" — literally "problem-solvers,"
      those who got ahead via test-based meritocracy) are
      systematically blocked from promotion by lower-credentialed but
      well-connected peers. Traditionally framed as a triple
      disadvantage (county + institution + frontline), this position
      is being structurally reframed in the AI era as a triple moat,
      because the work AI replaces fastest (coordination, reporting,
      meetings) is concentrated on the promotion track, and the work
      AI replaces slowest (contextual judgment, frontline execution,
      lived human knowledge) is concentrated at the blocked-frontline
      position.
    confidence: "high"
    confidence_basis: "K's 10+ years of direct observation and multi-peer validation"

  the_shared_script_of_county_test_takers:
    description: |
      A recurring sequence of experiences that K has observed in his
      county-level peers. Not universal but highly common.
    stages:
      - stage: "Entry high"
        description: "Passing the entrance exam is celebrated as the village's highlight moment"
      - stage: "Task assignment reveal"
        description: "High-credential workers get assigned documentation, equipment, and errand tasks; technical positions are already occupied by older hires"
      - stage: "Promotion reveal"
        description: "Merit is retroactive — performance is 'discovered' only when leadership already wants to promote someone"
      - stage: "Income reveal"
        description: "Salaries look similar on paper, but family wealth (shops, properties, rentals) creates massive real-income gaps"
      - stage: "Psychological erosion"
        description: "The organization trains 'people with ideas' into 'people who don't think much'"
      - stage: "Exit trap"
        description: "Credentials that were entry bonuses become expired goods when trying to leave"
    confidence: "high"
    confidence_basis: "Observed across 10+ years, cross-validated with peer narratives"
    boundary_conditions:
      - "Varies by specific industry within the county-level system"
      - "Less acute in organizations with more merit-based cultures (rare but exist)"
      - "Highly dependent on whether the individual has even minimal local connections"

  strategies_for_reversal:

    - name: "重新定义结算单位 (Redefine the unit of settlement)"
      description: |
        Stop measuring success by promotion (long-cycle, externally
        controlled, revokable). Start measuring by daily accumulation
        of three assets: time, information asymmetry, and real human
        relationships. Each settles daily, cannot be revoked, does
        not show up in the official record, and cannot be taxed or
        audited.
      confidence: "high"
      confidence_basis: "K's current operating mode, core ANSS principle"
      boundary_conditions:
        - "Requires psychological detachment from organizational status validation"
        - "Less effective if the daily job is so demanding it leaves no cognitive bandwidth"
        - "Requires an external project to absorb the accumulated assets (content, skills, network)"

    - name: "把岗位定义为带薪田野调查 (Reframe the job as paid fieldwork)"
      description: |
        The salary funds the research. The workplace is the field
        site. The daily work is data collection. The external project
        (ANSS, Cyber Lab content, future business) is the publication.
        Identity shifts from "employee" to "paid field researcher."
      confidence: "moderate_to_high"
      confidence_basis: "K's recent reframing, ~6 months tested"
      boundary_conditions:
        - "Only works if the worker has an external project that can absorb the data"
        - "Risk of visible detachment harming workplace performance and triggering scrutiny"
        - "The 'fieldwork' framing must not be verbalized at the workplace under any circumstances"

    - name: "押注一线知识的稀缺性 (Bet on frontline knowledge scarcity)"
      description: |
        Frontline knowledge — how things actually get done, who needs
        to be approached in what way, what unwritten rules govern
        outcomes — is precisely the knowledge AI cannot extract from
        documents. The blocked-from-promotion position keeps the
        worker close to this source. Promoted peers lose access the
        moment they leave the frontline. In the AI era, this reverses
        the hierarchy of knowledge value.
      confidence: "high"
      confidence_basis: "K's direct daily observation"
      boundary_conditions:
        - "Frontline knowledge has shelf life; organizational rules change"
        - "Knowledge must be actively documented to retain transferable value"
        - "Some frontline knowledge is highly industry-specific and non-transferable outside the context"

    - name: "将三重劣势视为三重护城河 (Reframe triple disadvantage as triple moat)"
      description: |
        Three traditional disadvantages become structural advantages
        in the AI era:
        - County location: low noise, low cost of living, immunity to
          tier-1 attention economy
        - Institutional employment: stable cash flow + large blocks
          of unsupervised time (the two most expensive resources for
          solo creators)
        - Frontline position: direct, first-hand, lived experience
          that AI cannot synthesize from public data
      confidence: "high"
      confidence_basis: "Structural reasoning combined with K's own resource audit"
      boundary_conditions:
        - "True only if the worker actively uses these resources for external accumulation"
        - "Institutional jobs vary — some leave no time, some are highly surveilled"
        - "County context does genuinely limit some opportunities (in-person professional networks, cutting-edge industry exposure)"

  key_insight:
    description: |
      In the AI era, proximity to actual operations becomes more
      valuable than proximity to organizational power. The promotion
      track in traditional Chinese organizations leads precisely
      toward the tasks AI replaces fastest (coordination, reporting,
      meetings, document production). The blocked-from-promotion
      frontline worker, by accident of structural exclusion, ends up
      holding the more AI-resilient position. The system that blocked
      them is paradoxically protecting them from the AI replacement
      wave hitting the promoted class.
    confidence: "moderate_to_high"
    confidence_basis: "Strong theoretical case with early empirical validation; long-term validation pending"
```

```yaml
mental_models:

  - name: "三重劣势三重护城河 (Triple Disadvantage Triple Moat)"
    description: |
      The same structural position (county + institution + frontline)
      produces different outcomes in different eras. In the
      information era it was a ceiling. In the AI era it is a moat.
      Era-dependent evaluation is essential; absolute assessments of
      "disadvantage" are trap thinking.
    application: "Re-audit any 'disadvantaged' position against current technological context"

  - name: "结算单位切换 (Switching the Settlement Unit)"
    description: |
      The single most important psychological move for a blocked
      institutional worker. The organization measures you in
      promotion units (5-10 year settlement, external control). You
      must measure yourself in daily-accumulation units (time,
      information, relationships — daily settlement, self-control).
      Same job, different scoreboard.
    application: "Apply to any long-hierarchy work environment"

  - name: "AI 替代梯度 (AI Replacement Gradient)"
    description: |
      Within a traditional organization, AI replacement risk
      correlates positively with organizational height. Roles
      focused on documents, coordination, meetings, and reporting
      replace fastest. Roles focused on contextual physical action,
      specific human judgment, and frontline execution replace
      slowest. The hierarchy and the AI risk gradient point in the
      same direction.
    application: "Map any organization's roles by AI replacement risk to identify hidden winners"

  - name: "带薪田野调查员 (Paid Field Researcher)"
    description: |
      Reframe employment as funded research. The salary is the
      grant. The workplace is the field site. The daily frustrations
      are data. The external project is the publication. Converts
      frustration into curiosity; converts stuck position into
      research access.
    application: "Apply to any mid-career worker in an organization they cannot immediately leave"

  - name: "三样拿不走的东西 (Three Unrevocable Assets)"
    description: |
      Time, information asymmetry, real human relationships. These
      are the assets that don't enter the official account, don't
      appear on resumes, cannot be audited, cannot be taxed, and
      cannot be revoked by organizational decisions. In the AI era,
      these three become the primary currency of individual
      sovereignty.
    application: "Daily asset tracking alongside (or instead of) traditional income tracking"
```

```yaml
    rationale: "Changing the system is high-cost, low-probability. Changing what you count is free and immediate."

  - principle: "Era shift reveals hidden advantage"

  - principle: "Proximity to operations beats proximity to power in disruption eras"
    rationale: "Power roles standardize first and get replaced first. Operational knowledge is tacit and replaces last."

  - principle: "Build what cannot be revoked"
    rationale: "Anything that can be given by an organization can be taken back. Build assets (time, information, relationships, skills) that exist outside organizational jurisdiction."

  - principle: "Stuck position = protected observation post"
    rationale: "Being blocked from advancement preserves your access to frontline reality. Advancement would have cost you your most valuable data source."
```

```yaml
failure_conditions:

  - name: "幻觉式突围 (Hallucinated breakout)"
    description: "Worker misreads the frame and quits immediately to chase AI-era opportunities without a stable external project or income floor. Result: loses institutional cover before new structure is built."
    frequency: "Common risk when the reversal insight first lands"
    response: "Keep institutional position until double-threshold met: side income ≥60% of family expenses for 3 months, AND 12 months cash reserves"

  - name: "田野数据外泄 (Field data exposure)"
    description: "Worker documents frontline observations in ways that expose specific organizations, colleagues, or incidents. Result: workplace retaliation, loss of access to field site, possible legal consequences."
    frequency: "High risk for anyone doing content work while employed"
    response: "Strict anonymization: no real unit names, no geography, no job titles, no policy evaluations, no time-specific events. Use group-perspective narration only."

  - name: "心智停留在旧单位 (Mental residence in the old unit)"
    description: "Worker intellectually adopts the new framework but emotionally still seeks validation from the organization. Result: frustration continues, accumulation does not."
    frequency: "Very common, hardest to detect in oneself"
    response: "Periodic self-check: what am I still hoping for from the organization that I should have already released?"

  - name: "把 AI 当魔法 (Treating AI as magic)"
    description: "Worker assumes AI will automatically convert frontline knowledge into valuable output. Result: frontline knowledge stays in head, AI has nothing to work with."
    frequency: "Common among AI-optimistic mid-career workers"
    response: "AI is a research team and a mirror, not a ghostwriter. The human must actively extract, document, and structure the frontline knowledge first. See K-LAB-103 for the triangulation workflow."

  - name: "护城河被识破 (Moat recognized and copied)"
    description: "As AI era matures, more frontline workers realize the same reversal, driving up competition for attention in this exact positioning. Result: the moat narrows."
    frequency: "Low today, rising over 3-5 years"
    response: "Early movers capture disproportionate advantage. Worker must move within 12-24 months while the positioning is uncommon."
```
